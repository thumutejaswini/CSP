<h1 style="text-align:center">agricultural courses</h1>
<p>Many countries like India, China, the USA, etc are heavily dependent on Agriculture. Not only does it help in the growth of the economy but it also generates massive employment opportunities. As a result of this, many universities offer degree programs in Agriculture at undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctorate levels. Here is a list of courses that you can pursue after BiPC in the field of Agriculture:</p
<li>BSc Agriculture</li>
<li>BSc in Horticulture</li>
<li>BSc in Plant Production</li>
<li>BSc in Animal Production</li>
<li>BSc in Entomology</li>
<li>BSc in Agricultural Biotechnology</li>
<li>Bachelor in International Food and Agribusiness</li>
<li>Bachelors in International Rural Innovation and Development</li>
<li>Bachelor in Horticulture and Business Management</li>
<h3>Agriculture Course Eligibility</h3>
<p>To enrol in the agriculture course, you must pass class 12 in the science stream with a minimum of 45% to 50% aggregate. Students need to meet this eligibility criterion to get admission to colleges and universities. If you want to study agriculture abroad, the same eligibility criteria apply to universities abroad in addition to English proficiency tests.</p>
