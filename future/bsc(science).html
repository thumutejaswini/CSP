BSC(SCIENCE)

BSc stands for Bachelor of Science is a 3-year undergraduate course that provides students with a strong foundation in the sciences. BSc Courses are offered in a variety of scientific disciplines such as BSc Nursing, BSc Physics, BSc Computer Science, etc.

\

Why Study BSc?

  • Strong Foundation in Science:

    BSc courses provide students with a strong foundation in the sciences. This foundation can be used to pursue a variety of careers in STEM fields.

  • High earning potential:

    BSc offers high earning potential, for example, students studying BSc chemistry, BSc Biochemistry, BSc Industrial Chemistry will have great opportunities in the field as the industry will rise from USD 179 Billion in 2019 to USD 304 Billion in 2025.

  • Job security:

    STEM occupations are projected to grow much faster than the average for all occupations over the next decade in a variety of industries, including healthcare, IT, and manufacturing.

  • Focused Field of Study:

    BSc allows students to concentrate their studies on one field or subject which provides them the opportunity to pursue further studies and research in the same field.

    BSc Courses: Eligibility Criteria

    • Candidates should clear Class 12 in the Science stream with a minimum of 50% - to 60% aggregate from a recognised board. It may be noted that the minimum percentage required for BSc admissions may vary depending on the policy of the university/ college to which a candidate is applying.
    • Candidates must have studied (PCM) Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics or (PCB) (Physics, Chemistry and Biology) as core subject combinations.
    • Generally, there is no age limit for pursuing BSc courses unless specified by an institute's eligibility criteria.
