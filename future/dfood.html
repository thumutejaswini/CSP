Food Technology

Food processing and preservation technology have been increasingly popular in recent years, and a diploma in Food Technology is a programme that covers all of the necessary disciplines. Students who choose to pursue a diploma in food technology can do so after completing their 10th and 12th grades. The diploma level will be higher after the 12th.

Some of the topics addressed in this curriculum include microbiology, enzyme technology, food hygiene and sanitation, nutrition and health, food processing, food plant layout and design, laws, quality assurance, and unit operations in food processing.

When students first start their employment as a basic-level food technologist, Students can expect to earn INR 4 lakhs per year. Food Technology Jobs is a good place to start. Lab technician, biochemist, food technologist, and production manager are just a few of the jobs available in the field of food technology. A food technologist in India earns an average of INR 4,18,000 a year, according to PayScale.

Diploma in Food Technology Admission

There are lots of universities and colleges which offer this diploma course. Those students who want to apply for this Diploma in Food Technology must have 10th degrees and the age limit is 15 years. Students must have 50% score in their previous degree. This article discloses all the information related to Diploma in Food Technology course.

Among the topics covered in this diploma program are food chemistry, food microbiology, food technology, food packaging techniques, food processing, food materials, dairy technology, cereal technology, cost management, accounting, and sensory evaluation methods.

Diploma in Food Technology Admission Eligibility Criteria

Those aspirants who wish to apply for this Diploma in Food Technology course must have match eligibility.

  • Students must have to 10th standard (with 50%-55%) through the recognized board and institute.
  • Candidate must have studied in science stream. (main subjects must be chemistry, physics, and biology)
  • 12th-level certification must be obtained from a recognized board in India.
  • There are no age criteria.
